We are well aware of the challenges that EB-5 investors face due to the USCIS' bureaucratic delays, which cause uncertainty about the future and prevent investors and their families from fully deriving the benefits of their conditional permanent resident status.
A mandamus case is a lawsuit in a Federal Court under the Mandamus Act to compel the government agency to perform their duty as required by law.
There are many factors to be considered in a mandamus case as in any other litigation. We believe the best way forward is to proceed in the U.S. District Court in Washington D.C., where USCIS is located. They are familiar with these types of lawsuits and know how to handle these cases.
The advantage of our team over other firms is that we not only have a USCIS background but also have experience with filing these type of cases. Our strategy has been very successful: we received over 250 approvals for EB-5 investors and their family members after filing the complaint in a Federal Court in Washington D.C.
As part of the strategy, we file mandamus cases on behalf of a group of investors, as joint representation provides savings over the costs. Please contact us to learn more.
Mandamus cases are not often brought up because they require a lot of preparation and diligence. Filing general USCIS matters is not the same. It requires highly specialized advocacy skills and not many immigration attorneys know how to file a strong mandamus case. You can fully rely on our expertise.
In our experience, on average, it takes 4 months after filing the case to get a Green Card in hand. However, USCIS may send a Request for Evidence for the I-829 petition beforehand, for example, requesting to supplement the I-829 petition with absent tax forms for the partnership. That may delay the Green Card delivery as it depends on the availability of requested documents.
We have helped over 250 investors and their family members who did not experience any retaliation and were approved within several months after filing a mandamus complaint. We will be happy to discuss it with you further.

ATTORNEY
Attorney Pavel Paromov
Pavel advises clients with respect to investment immigration matters. His core practice is focused on the EB-5 visa program, which comprises commercial contracting, corporate governance, franchise laws, financial regulations, and litigation. In other words, Pavel helps foreign investors chart efficient paths to obtain Green Cards in the United States.

Pavel started his legal career in 1998, when as a junior at law school in Russia, he joined a group of students who were offered positions at the Human Rights Committee. At the Committee, he provided legal work, mostly assisting indigent clients. Helping people solve their legal problems quickly turned into a passion for Pavel. Upon graduating from law school, Pavel decided to continue his career as a defense attorney. It has been a very challenging but rewarding experience. His commitment, hard work, and persistence has led to justice being done. Justice inspires him.

Due to personal circumstances, he immigrated to the United States. In order to proceed with his professional career, he applied for and was admitted to Boston University School of Law. Pavel completed two Masters of Laws programs  International Business Transactions and Banking and Financial Laws. Attending law school in the United States not only advanced his legal knowledge significantly, but also enabled Pavel to gain a comprehensive understanding of the central tenets of the law as it is practiced in the United States. With his academic and professional background firmly established, he aspires to use his acquired knowledge and experience to assist his clients with the highest level of service. His bilingual proficiency, as well as his personal immigrant experience, is what truly sets him apart from American-born colleagues and allows him to represent not only English-speaking clients but also those who speak English as a second language. He knows what it feels like to be a "new American", to learn a new language, and to start all over again. Pavel can easily relate to confusions associated with adjustment to a new realm. This helps him to establish effective communication on a much more personal level with clients, who (while maybe fluent in conversational English) are not familiar with legal terminology and, as a result, can fall victim to a complex legal system that they do not understand.

Honorable William P. Joyce
Honorable William P. Joyce Esq. retired as an Immigration Judge in the United States Immigration Court in July 2002. Prior to becoming an Immigration Judge, Judge Joyce served as Legal Counsel to the Chief Immigration Judge who supervised 210 Immigration Judges throughout the United States. In that capacity, Judge Joyce worked closely with other branches in the Justice Department and Congress in complex legal matters affecting the Immigration Court.
Judge Joyce was also an Assistant United States Attorney of the Eastern District of Virginia, the Associate General Counsel of INS, and worked under Bill Weld at the United States Attorney Office in Boston. He is a graduate of the Georgetown University School of Foreign Service and the Georgetown University Law School.
